Tricks to memorizing matrix elements of Ladder operators

November 19, 2016

I still remember how surprised I was when I was learning a simple harmonic oscillator’s Ladder operators, $a$ and $a^\dagger$, in Prof. Chiang’s summer quantum mechanics class. But for a long time, I did not know how to remember the matrix elements of Ladder operators, including $J_\pm$ in angular momentum theories.

$$ a|n\rangle=\sqrt{n}|n-1\rangle $$ $$ a^\dagger|n\rangle=\sqrt{n+1}|n+1\rangle $$

$$ J_\pm|j, m\rangle = \sqrt{(j\pm m+1)(j\mp m)}\hbar|j, m\pm 1\rangle $$

In other words, I did not know what were the coefficients in front of the new state ket.

This week, I finally found some simple tricks to memorize those $\sqrt{n}$, $\sqrt{n+1}$, and especially, $\sqrt{(j\pm m+1)(j\mp m)}$. That is,

$$ O|n\rangle=\sqrt{\max(n, n’)}|n’\rangle $$

$O$ represents $a$ or $a^\dagger$ and $n’$ is $n-1$ or $n+1$. Similarly, for $J_\pm$,

$$ J_{\pm}|j, m\rangle = \sqrt{(j+\max(m, m’))(j-\min(n, n’))}\hbar|j, m’\rangle $$

and $m’$ is $m-1$ or $m+1$, depending on the operator you applied on $|j, m\rangle$.

One can easily verify that the last two equations are equivalent to the original formulas.
